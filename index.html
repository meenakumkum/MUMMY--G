    Frequently Asked Questioins

    Doubt clearance play an important role for questions during pregnancy period for a women, here are some Frequently asked questions related to maternal health and pregnancy.

    • When does a woman check if she is pregnant?

      A woman should check within a time period of 14-21 days after she skipped her periods.

    • How much percent accurate are the pregnancy testing kits?

      Prega kits are 99% accurate in testing the pregnancy of a woman who skipped her periods.

    • What are the early signs of pregnancy — and when will I notice them?

      The most common sign of pregnancy is a missing period, but you might also experience nausea, heartburn, constipation, tiredness, or breast tenderness. These can occur within the first six to eight weeks of pregnancy.

    • What are the odds of a false positive or a false negative on a pregnancy test?

      In very uncommon cases, a miscarriage, some cancers, or certain medications may create a false positive result, she said, but most of the time, a positive result means you're pregnant. It's very unlikely to have a false positive.

    • Is it really that dangerous to get pregnant after 35?

      When women get pregnant in their late 30s or 40s, there's a higher risk for certain complications, including birth defects, miscarriage, stillbirth, multiple pregnancy (like twins or triplets).

    • Is it safe to exercise during pregnancy?

      For regular healthy women who are having a low-risk pregnancy, exercise is actually recommended.
